# Empirical Country Rankings on the Low-Context/High-Context Communication Scale: Evidence and Data Sources

## 1. Introduction

A central challenge in cross-cultural business research is finding robust, quantitative data to empirically rank countries on Erin Meyer's "Communication" scale (Low Context ↔ High Context), as described in "The Culture Map." While Hall's original theory and much of the literature provide qualitative or anecdotal classifications, several recent studies have attempted to operationalize and measure this dimension quantitatively. The most direct empirical sources include Shen (2022), who provides explicitness scores for 55 countries using a linguistic corpus of UN speeches; Bojadjiev et al. (2023), who report communication style factor scores for 10 countries; Vescovi et al. (2022), who validate a low-high context scale across China, Israel, and the USA; and Kim et al. (1998), who empirically confirm Hall's framework for China, Korea, and the USA  (Shen, 2022; Bojadjiev et al., 2023; Vescovi et al., 2022; Kim et al., 1998). Additional proxies—such as Hofstede's Individualism-Collectivism index—are widely used but are not perfect substitutes for direct measurement  (Taras et al., 2012; Taras et al., 2010; Akaliyski et al., 2025). Below is a synthesis of the best available empirical data sources and country rankings that can be mapped to Meyer's axis.

## 3. Results

### 3.1 Direct Empirical Country Rankings

#### A) Shen (2022): Explicitness of Persuasion Scores (UN General Debate Corpus)
- **Method**: Linguistic analysis of UN speeches from 55 countries over 51 years.
- **Scale**: Higher score = more explicit (lower context); lower score = more implicit (higher context).
- **Sample Data**:
    - **Highest Explicitness (Low Context)**: United States, Canada, Australia, UK
    - **Lowest Explicitness (High Context)**: China, South Korea, Japan
    - **Intermediate**: France, Germany
- **Access**: Full country list and scores available in supplementary materials ( (Shen, 2022)).

#### B) Bojadjiev et al. (2023): Communication Style Factor Scores
- **Method**: Survey-based factor analysis across 10 countries.
- **Scale**: Higher score = more professional/direct/explicit; lower score = more casual/implicit.
- **Countries & Scores**:
    - Germany: Highest professional/direct
    - Japan: Most casual/implicit
    - Other countries ranked: Sweden, China, Russia, Italy, USA, UK, Serbia, North Macedonia ( (Bojadjiev et al., 2023)).

#### C) Vescovi et al. (2022): LC-HC Scale Validation
- **Method**: Developed a reliable scale measuring contextual reasoning/social relations.
- **Countries Measured**: China (high context), Israel (medium), USA (low context)
- **Scores**: Not reported on a 0–99 scale but show clear ranking ( (Vescovi et al., 2022)).

#### D) Kim et al. (1998): Empirical Confirmation of Hall’s Framework
- **Countries Measured**: China & Korea (high context), USA (low context)
- **Findings**: Consistent with Hall’s predictions ( (Kim et al., 1998)).

### 3.2 Proxies Using Cultural Dimensions

#### E) Hofstede Individualism–Collectivism Index
- Widely used as a proxy; higher individualism ≈ lower context.
- Meta-analysis by Taras et al. (2012) provides updated scores for 49 countries ( (Taras et al., 2012)).
    - Example scores:
        - USA ~91/100 (very low context)
        - Japan ~46/100
        - China ~20/100 (very high context)

#### F) GLOBE Study Assertiveness/In-group Collectivism
- Provides additional proxies but not direct LC-HC measures ( (House et al., 2002; Rafferty, 2025; Nadeem & Luque, 2020)).

### 3.3 Other Notable Empirical Studies

- Hornikx & le Pair (2017): Belgium vs Netherlands—Belgium higher context than Netherlands ( (Hornikx & Pair, 2017)).
- Alizadeh Afrouzi (2021): Switzerland vs Colombia—Switzerland lower context than Colombia ( (Afrouzi, 2021)).
- Broeder (2021): Dutch/Japanese/Greek comparison with survey-based classification ( (Broeder, 2021)).
- Usunier & Roulin (2010): Web content analysis classifies countries into high/low/medium context groups ( (Usunier & Roulin, 2010)).

## 4. Discussion

Empirical measurement of national communication styles remains challenging due to conceptual ambiguities and methodological limitations  (Kittler et al., 2011; Cardon, 2008). However, several recent studies have made significant progress by developing validated scales or leveraging large-scale linguistic corpora  (Shen, 2022; Bojadjiev et al., 2023; Vescovi et al., 2022). Shen’s corpus-based explicitness index offers perhaps the closest direct mapping to Meyer's Communication scale across a broad set of countries  (Shen, 2022). Bojadjiev et al.'s factor scores provide another promising approach but cover fewer countries  (Bojadjiev et al., 2023). Most other studies either focus on small samples or use proxies such as Hofstede’s Individualism–Collectivism index  (Taras et al., 2012), which correlates with but does not fully capture the LC-HC dimension.

There is consensus that East Asian cultures such as China/Japan/Korea are high-context communicators while Anglo cultures like the US/UK/Australia are low-context  (Shen, 2022; Kim et al., 1998; Afrouzi, 2021). European nations tend to fall in between or show internal variation  (Hornikx & Pair, 2017; Usunier & Roulin, 2010). However, many classic country rankings are based on anecdotal evidence rather than robust data  (Kittler et al., 2011; Broeder, 2021).

## 5. Conclusion

While no single study provides a complete global ranking from 0–99 directly matching Meyer's Communication scale for all countries, several recent works offer robust empirical data that can be mapped onto this axis—most notably Shen’s explicitness index for 55 nations and Bojadjiev et al.’s factor scores for ten major economies. Hofstede’s Individualism–Collectivism remains a widely used proxy where direct measures are unavailable.
